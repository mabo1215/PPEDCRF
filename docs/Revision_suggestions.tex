\subsection*{Remaining Editorial Items}

\subsubsection*{E1. Clarify ``earlier apparent instability'' in the Conclusion}
The conclusion states:
\begin{quote}
``Seed averaging substantially reduces the earlier apparent instability in the main protocol\ldots''
\end{quote}
A cold reader encountering this manuscript for the first time does not know what ``earlier'' refers to---it is an artifact of the revision history rather than a reference to something explained in the paper. The single-run results that showed apparent instability are not discussed in the paper.

\textbf{Recommended fix:}
Replace with:
\begin{quote}
Seed averaging across three independent noise realizations stabilizes the transfer assessment in the main protocol\ldots
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{E2. Align the ACM abstract transfer sentence with the body}
The ACM abstract says:
\begin{quote}
``Transfer across the eight-backbone seed-averaged benchmark is broadly supportive\ldots''
\end{quote}
This is vaguer than the body text, which quantifies ``23 of 24 backbone--gallery cells show negative $\Delta$.'' Adding the count to the abstract would strengthen the reader's first impression without adding length.

\textbf{Recommended fix:}
\begin{quote}
Transfer across the eight-backbone seed-averaged benchmark is broadly supportive (23 of 24 backbone--gallery cells show negative~$\Delta$)\ldots
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{E3. Unify ``broadly supportive'' vs.\ ``broadly favorable'' in Section 3.4}
After prior revisions, Section~3.4 now contains two near-synonymous phrases in close proximity:
\begin{itemize}
    \item ``broadly supportive transfer'' (in the main interpretation paragraph),
    \item ``broadly favorable transfer'' (in the transition sentence).
\end{itemize}
Using two slightly different adjectives for the same concept may look like an oversight.

\textbf{Recommended fix:}
Standardize both to ``broadly supportive transfer'' for phrase stability.

\subsubsection*{E4. Consider adding $\pm$std to PSNR/SSIM in the matched-operating-point table}
In Table~3 (matched), stochastic rows now correctly show Top-1 as $\text{mean}\pm\text{std}$ (including $\pm 0.000$). However, the PSNR and SSIM columns for those same stochastic rows are still reported as exact scalars (e.g., \texttt{36.14}, \texttt{30.16}), even though Table~1 (ablation) reports corresponding values as $36.14\pm0.01$ and $0.918\pm0.001$.

This cross-table inconsistency is very minor (the std is $\approx$0.01\,dB), and adding $\pm$std may clutter the already dense matched table. Either adding it or adding a short table footnote acknowledging the omission would resolve the issue.

\textbf{Recommended fix (minimal):}
Add one footnote sentence to the matched table caption:
\begin{quote}
PSNR and SSIM columns report stochastic means; their seed-to-seed standard deviations ($\leq$0.01\,dB and $\leq$0.001 respectively) are omitted for readability.
\end{quote}
