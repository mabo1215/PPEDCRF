\section*{ACM TOMM Final Quality Check -- Independent Review}

\subsection*{Target Venue}
ACM Transactions on Multimedia Computing, Communications, and Applications (ACM TOMM).

\subsection*{Overall Recommendation}
\textbf{Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions (editorial only)}

\subsection*{Overall Assessment}
The manuscript is now at a mature ACM TOMM submission level. The contribution is clearly scoped: DCRF-inferred support localization is positioned as the most transferable component, and PPEDCRF is framed as a calibrated operating-point selector rather than a universally superior privacy defense. The experimental evidence covers eight attacker backbones, three noise seeds, matched-operating-point analysis, temporal-consistency metrics, and a 50-pair appendix scaling confirmation. The paper is internally consistent and does not overclaim.

The remaining issues are all editorial or stylistic. No new experiments or structural changes are needed. The review below identifies seven minor items that, if addressed, will make the paper read more cleanly under ACM TOMM reviewer scrutiny.

\subsection*{Detailed Revision Items}

\subsubsection*{E1. Reduce repetition of ``substantially'' in the Conclusion [Minor]}
The word ``substantially'' appears three times within the four-paragraph Conclusion (lines~1070, 1071, 1073). This makes the conclusion read slightly promotional. Replace at least one occurrence with a neutral alternative or rephrase the sentence.

\textbf{Suggested fix:}
In the second paragraph, change ``PPEDCRF's practical advantage is preserving substantially higher utility at the same nominal noise scale'' to ``PPEDCRF's practical advantage is preserving higher utility at the same nominal noise scale.'' The $\approx$6\,dB figure already conveys the magnitude; ``substantially'' is redundant.

\subsubsection*{E2. Vary the phrase ``broadly supportive'' [Minor]}
The phrase ``broadly supportive'' appears in the abstract, the robustness analysis, and the conclusion. While the meaning is correct, the repetition makes it sound like a stock phrase. Change at least one instance to a different formulation, such as ``show consistent negative $\Delta$'' or ``demonstrate transfer across diverse attacker families.''

\subsubsection*{E3. Clarify ``most transferable component'' claim [Minor]}
The paper states twice that ``support localization is the most transferable component.'' While this is supported by the matched-operating-point convergence and the cross-backbone negative-$\Delta$ pattern, the claim sounds like a ranking across many dimensions. Consider softening the first occurrence (in the matched-OP discussion, around line~956) to: ``confirming that support localization is the most consistently beneficial component in the current evaluation.'' The conclusion version can remain as is.

\subsubsection*{E4. Fix slight conflation in the matched-OP section [Minor]}
Around line~945, the text states: ``confirming that Gaussian noise is a more privacy-effective perturbation than blur when applied to the same DCRF support.'' This conflates the perturbation type with the support localization. Gaussian noise is more privacy-effective than blur at 30\,dB PSNR \emph{because it destroys feature structure more aggressively}, not because of support. Rephrase to: ``confirming that stochastic Gaussian noise disrupts retrieval features more effectively than deterministic blur at similar perceptual degradation levels.''

\subsubsection*{E5. Remove ``lightweight'' from ``lightweight dynamic CRF'' [Minor]}
Around line~380, the DCRF is described as ``lightweight'' without runtime evidence.  Since the paper does not report inference time, drop the modifier or replace it with ``a small number of mean-field iterations'' to be precise.

\subsubsection*{E6. Consolidate the ``12-pair'' and ``12 paired locations'' inconsistency [Minor]}
The Conclusion uses both ``12 paired locations'' (paragraph~3) and ``12-pair benchmark'' (elsewhere). Pick one canonical form---preferably ``12-pair''---and use it consistently in the Conclusion and robustness analysis. The full form ``12 paired locations'' should appear only when first introduced in the experimental protocol.

\subsubsection*{E7. Deduplicate the repeated temporal-ablation observation [Minor]}
The temporal-consistency section (around lines~1042--1048) contains two sentences that say nearly the same thing:
\begin{quote}
``...the difference between full PPEDCRF and its temporal ablation is negligible on these short clips. On the short clips used here, differences between full PPEDCRF and the temporal ablation are small...''
\end{quote}
These are clearly a duplication artifact. Delete the second sentence.
