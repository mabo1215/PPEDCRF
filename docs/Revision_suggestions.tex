\section*{Independent Review -- ACM TOMM Submission Readiness}

\textbf{This review is written from scratch based solely on the current manuscript (\texttt{paper/main.tex}, \texttt{paper/appendix.tex}) and rendered figures, evaluated against ACM Transactions on Multimedia Computing, Communications, and Applications (ACM TOMM) standards. It has no connection to any prior review cycle.}

\subsection*{Overall Assessment}
The manuscript presents PPEDCRF, a release-side selective perturbation framework that concentrates Gaussian noise on DCRF-inferred location-sensitive background regions to protect video-frame geo-privacy while preserving perceptual quality. The paper is well-structured, honestly scoped, and technically sound. The eight-backbone seed-averaged benchmark with matched-operating-point analysis is thorough by multimedia systems standards. The contribution positioning as a ``calibrated privacy--utility trade-off optimizer'' rather than a universal privacy solution is appropriate.

\textbf{Recommendation:} Accept with minor revisions. The items below are editorial or presentational; none requires new experiments.

\subsection*{Components That Are Publication-Ready}
The following should not be changed:
\begin{itemize}
    \item problem formulation, threat model, and non-DP scope disclaimer,
    \item DCRF--NCP--Gaussian pipeline description (Eq.~1--5, Algorithm~1),
    \item seed-averaged eight-backbone benchmark protocol,
    \item matched-operating-point analysis and temporal-consistency evaluation,
    \item Related Work coverage (seven paragraphs, 42 references),
    \item appendix 50-pair scaling confirmation,
    \item bounded claim framing and MixVPR exception acknowledgment.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Actionable Revision Items}

\subsubsection*{R1. Fix overlapping $\sigma$ labels in Fig.~3 (privacy--utility frontier)}
In the rendered figure \texttt{figs/privacy\_utility\_tradeoff.jpg}, the sigma annotations for PPEDCRF and global Gaussian noise overlap at convergence points around 30\,dB PSNR in the R@1 panel. The overlapping labels read as ``$\sigma=86$'' instead of two separate annotations. This obscures an important result: that the two methods converge at matched PSNR.

\textbf{Required fix:}
Regenerate the figure with vertical or horizontal offset between the two $\sigma$ label sets (e.g., place PPEDCRF labels above the marker and global-noise labels below, or use different annotation colors).

\subsubsection*{R2. Explain apparent non-selectivity in Fig.~6 qualitative panel}
Fig.~6 panel~(d) shows the absolute pixel difference map at $\sigma_0=24$. It appears nearly uniform red across the entire frame---including the foreground pedestrians and ground---which visually contradicts the paper's central narrative of \emph{selective} perturbation. This is because $\sigma_0=24$ is a strong perturbation level where even the ``unperturbed'' foreground regions show visible noise spillover after clipping.

The text says ``$\sigma_0=24$ for visual clarity,'' but the figure actually undercuts the selectivity argument by showing barely any spatial structure in the difference map.

\textbf{Required fix (choose one):}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Add one sentence to the caption explicitly noting that $\sigma_0=24$ is chosen for visibility and that lower-$\sigma$ settings produce sharper spatial selectivity; or
    \item Regenerate the figure at $\sigma_0=8$ or $\sigma_0=16$ where the difference map shows clearer spatial localization, and adjust the caption accordingly.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{R3. Add a brief note on computational overhead}
The paper gives no indication of runtime cost. For ACM TOMM, which targets multimedia systems, even one sentence about practical throughput would help. Reviewers from the multimedia-systems community will ask about this.

\textbf{Required fix:}
Add one sentence in the Experimental Protocol section (or after Algorithm~1) stating approximate per-frame processing time on the hardware used, or at least the number of DCRF iterations and whether the pipeline runs at interactive rates.

\subsubsection*{R4. Harmonize the two abstracts}
The ACM abstract (ELSE branch) now includes the concrete ``23 of 24'' quantification and is well-scoped. The IEEE abstract (IF branch) is noticeably weaker: it omits the 23/24 count, the appendix confirmation, and uses vaguer phrasing. While ACM TOMM will render the ELSE branch, maintaining both abstracts at the same quality avoids accidental regressions if the format flag changes.

\textbf{Suggested fix:}
Update the IEEE abstract to include the same ``23 of 24 backbone--gallery cells'' quantification.

\subsubsection*{R5. Tighten the Section 3.2 benchmark-hardness paragraph}
The paragraph beginning ``The mined benchmark is intentionally difficult\ldots'' provides useful context, but it includes raw floating-point statistics (mean similarity 0.989, range 0.980--0.997, mean max-sim 0.919, 0.856, hardest 0.994) in running prose. These would be easier to absorb in a compact inline display or a small supplementary table, freeing the prose to focus on the qualitative point: hard negatives, not random.

\textbf{Suggested fix:}
Either (a)~round the statistics (e.g., ``average cosine similarity $\approx$0.99'') and reduce detail, or (b)~move the full statistics to parenthetical or end-of-section display.

\subsubsection*{R6. Caption for Fig.~2 (architecture diagram)}
The current caption reads: ``Implementation PPEDCRF pipeline.'' The word ``Implementation'' as a standalone adjective is awkward.

\textbf{Suggested fix:}
Replace with ``PPEDCRF release-side pipeline'' or ``Overview of the PPEDCRF pipeline.''

\subsection*{Non-Actionable Observations (No Changes Needed)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The ablation showing temporal and NCP components are weakly distinguished is honestly presented and does not require additional experiments, given the paper's scope disclaimer.
    \item The matched-OP convergence (PPEDCRF = global noise at equal PSNR) is correctly reported and the ``parameter efficiency'' framing is appropriate for the current contribution claim.
    \item The 12-pair benchmark size is a real limitation, but it is adequately managed by the explicit disclaimers and the 50-pair appendix confirmation.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Bottom Line}
The manuscript is submission-ready for ACM TOMM. Six items remain, all editorial or presentational. The scientific contribution---a calibrated, DCRF-guided selective perturbation framework with bounded, carefully evaluated transfer---is appropriate for TOMM's multimedia privacy scope.
