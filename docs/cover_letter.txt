Cover Letter

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "PPEDCRF: Dynamic-CRF-Guided Selective Perturbation for Background-Based Location Privacy in Video Sequences" for your consideration.

Mobile-recorded videos -- from dashcams, visual-based ADAS platforms, bodycams, drones, and wearable cameras -- are increasingly shared for safety auditing, incident investigation, and data-driven model improvement. Even after explicit GPS metadata are stripped, an attacker can still infer the recording location by matching background visual cues -- buildings, road layouts, signage, skylines -- against geo-tagged reference imagery. Existing visual anonymization techniques focus on removing foreground identities (faces, license plates) and do not directly address this background-based location leakage.

Our manuscript proposes PPEDCRF, a selective perturbation framework that addresses this gap through three tightly integrated components: (1) a dynamic conditional random field (DCRF) that estimates location-sensitive background regions with temporal smoothing across frames, (2) a normalized control penalty (NCP) that rescales perturbation strength per pixel according to the inferred sensitivity, and (3) a calibrated Gaussian noise injection mechanism that perturbs only the inferred regions while preserving the visual quality needed for downstream perception tasks.

The key contributions of this work are:

- A unified computational pipeline for location-sensitive background sanitization with implementation-consistent notation and clearly defined inputs and outputs.
- A DP-style calibration mechanism that maps a user-chosen privacy budget to a base noise scale, with an explicit scope clarification distinguishing it from formal end-to-end differential privacy guarantees.
- A reproducible controlled paired-scene retrieval benchmark with eight attacker backbones and three noise seeds, together with ablation studies, privacy-utility frontier analysis, matched-operating-point comparisons, and attacker-sensitivity experiments.

On the paired-scene benchmark, PPEDCRF reduces ResNet18 Top-1 retrieval accuracy from 0.667 to 0.361 +/- 0.127 while preserving 36.14 dB PSNR -- an approximately 6 dB quality advantage over global Gaussian noise at comparable privacy. Support-aware baselines operating on the same inferred spatial masks push privacy further (mask-guided blur: 0.500 Top-1 at 30.47 dB; mask-guided mosaic: 0.167 Top-1 at 25.03 dB), demonstrating that the spatial support inferred by the DCRF module is itself a valuable contribution to privacy-preserving video release. Seed-averaged transfer across all eight attacker backbones shows consistent privacy improvement in 23 of 24 backbone-gallery cells.

This manuscript has not been published previously, is not under consideration elsewhere, and all authors have approved the submission. The source code and benchmark scripts are publicly available to support reproducibility.

We believe this work is well suited for your readership given its focus on privacy-preserving multimedia processing, visual content protection, and the intersection of computer vision with security.

Thank you for your time and consideration. We look forward to hearing from you.

Sincerely,

Author
